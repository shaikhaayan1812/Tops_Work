Session 4 - Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC)

1. Six SDLC Phases

Requirement - Gather customer needs.
Design - Plan the software structure.
Development - Write the code.
Testing - Find and fix bugs.
Deployment - Release software to users.
Maintenance - Update and improve the software.

2. Agile vs Waterfall

Agile - Flexible, Frequent releases, Customer feedback during development
Waterfall - Fixed process, One final release, Feedback mostly at the end

3. Scrum Roles

Product Owner (PO) - Defines product requirements and priorities.
Scrum Master - Helps the team follow Scrum practices.
Development Team - Designs, develops, and tests the software.

4. Sprint

A Sprint is a short development cycle where a team completes planned work.

Typical Duration: 1-4 weeks (commonly 2 weeks).

5. Jira Board Columns

Backlog - Future tasks waiting to be started
To Do - Ready to begin
In Progress - Currently being worked on
Done - Completed work
